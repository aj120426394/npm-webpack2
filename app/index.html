Constructive Alignment

John Biggs, an Australian, who went to live and work in England, theorised that for learning to be most effective for the students in our classes we needed to design that learning with a focus on aligning all of the elements of the learning; in his theory this includes:

  • what we want them to learn (Learning Outcomes)
  • how we are going to determine if they have learned it (assessment), and
  • the way we are going ot go about offering them opportunities to learn those things (learning activities).

Biggs, J. (2003) Aligning teaching for constructing learning, The Higher Education Academy. Found at https://www.heacademy.ac.uk/aligning-teaching-constructing-learning March, 10, 2017

This three pager gives a concise overview of ‘constructive alignment’ (CA)
